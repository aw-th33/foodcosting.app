# foodcosting.app — 12-Month Business Plan

**Last updated:** April 18, 2026
**Owner:** Ahmed
**Status:** Active working document

---

## Premise

This is not a fundraising document. This is an operating plan for a solo founder with limited weekly hours, supported by automated agent workflows. Every milestone assumes organic-first distribution and no paid acquisition until unit economics prove it viable.

---

- MVP is live and functional
- SEO keywords trending from 40+ to 20+ positions
- Community engagement (Facebook groups) producing early signups
- Content pipeline scaffolded with 7 scheduled agents
- Pricing: $15/month, $149/year, $99 limited lifetime deal
- Fixed costs: ~$150–200/month
- Paying customers: TBD (track this number weekly starting now)

## 12-Month Milestones

### Phase 1: Validation (Months 1–3 / April–June 2026)

**Goal:** Prove that organic distribution can produce paying customers at a sustainable rate.

| Milestone | Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Paying customers | 50 | Stripe dashboard |
| MRR | $500–750 | Stripe dashboard |
| Organic traffic | 3,000 visits/month | PostHog + GSC |
| Blog posts published | 20 total (8 new) | Notion blog database |
| Community responses posted | 40+ | Manual log |
| Email list | 200 subscribers | Email platform |
| SEO keywords in top 20 | 10+ terms | GSC |
| Churn rate baseline | Measured | Stripe cohort data |

**Key actions:**
- Publish 2 blog posts/week via content pipeline (agents draft, Ahmed reviews)
- Post in 3–5 Facebook groups and 2–3 subreddits weekly
- Launch email capture on the site (lead magnet: free food cost template or calculator)
- Set up basic churn tracking in PostHog or Stripe
- Kill the lifetime deal after this phase — it undermines LTV

**Decision gate (end of Month 3):**
- If <20 customers: Diagnose. Is it traffic? Conversion? Wrong audience? Adjust before Phase 2.
- If 20–50 customers: Proceed to Phase 2 with current approach.
- If 50+ customers: Accelerate Phase 2 timeline.

---

### Phase 2: Traction (Months 4–6 / July–September 2026)

**Goal:** Build repeatable organic acquisition and establish pricing power.

| Milestone | Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Paying customers | 150 | Stripe |
| MRR | $1,500–2,250 | Stripe |
| Organic traffic | 6,000 visits/month | PostHog + GSC |
| Blog posts published | 35 total | Notion |
| Free tool users (calculators) | 500/month | PostHog |
| Annual plan adoption | 30%+ of new signups | Stripe |
| Social media following | 500 combined | Platform analytics |

**Key actions:**
- Build 2–3 free calculator tools (cake pricing, food truck cost, catering quote) as SEO magnets
- Push annual plans as default pricing ($149/year = $12.42/month, framed as a discount)
- Begin testing price increase to $19/month for new monthly signups
- Launch short-form video pipeline (YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels) — 3–5/week
- Start guest posting or link-building via food business blogs
- Add one in-app conversion optimization (e.g., trial-to-paid nudge, onboarding improvement)

**Decision gate (end of Month 6):**
- If <100 customers: Serious reassessment. Consider pivoting price up aggressively ($25/month) or narrowing niche further.
- If 100–150: On track. Proceed to Phase 3.
- If 150+: Consider limited paid acquisition testing in Phase 3.

---

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 7–9 / October–December 2026)

**Goal:** Scale what's working. Introduce second revenue tier.

| Milestone | Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Paying customers | 350 | Stripe |
| MRR | $4,500–6,000 | Stripe |
| Organic traffic | 10,000 visits/month | PostHog + GSC |
| SEO keywords in top 10 | 10+ terms | GSC |
| Monthly churn | <5% | Stripe cohort data |
| Annual plan adoption | 40%+ | Stripe |

**Key actions:**
- Raise monthly price to $19/month (grandfather existing customers at $15)
- Introduce a "Pro" tier at $29–39/month with features like: multi-location, team access, PDF exports, or supplier price tracking
- Test one paid channel cautiously (Google Ads on high-intent keywords, $500/month budget cap)
- Expand SEO into adjacent keywords (restaurant profit margins, food business startup costs)
- Build case studies from early customers (social proof for conversion)
- Explore partnerships: POS integrations, food supplier referrals

**Decision gate (end of Month 9):**
- If <200 customers: Paid acquisition is not viable at this price. Focus purely on organic and consider whether the niche is too small.
- If 200–350: Healthy growth. Continue compounding.
- If 350+: Ahead of plan. Evaluate hiring first contractor or expanding to adjacent markets.

**Goal:** Reach sustainable revenue and establish the business as a going concern.

| Milestone | Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|

| MRR | $7,500–12,000 | Stripe |

**Key actions:**

- Consider international expansion (UK, Canada, Australia — English-speaking markets)
- Evaluate whether the product needs engineering investment beyond Lovable
- Plan Year 2 strategy based on what's actually working vs. what's not

## What We Are NOT Doing

These are explicitly out of scope for the next 12 months unless data forces a change:

- **Raising money.** The economics work without it. Capital adds pressure, not leverage.
- **Hiring full-time.** Agents + Ahmed's hours are sufficient through Phase 3. First hire (if any) is a contractor in Phase 4.
- **Building an enterprise product.** Stay SMB. Stay simple. The temptation to go upmarket will come — resist it until $10K MRR.
- **Paid acquisition at scale.** Small tests in Phase 3, not before. Organic must be the engine.
- **Feature development beyond conversion optimization.** The product is good enough. Distribution is the constraint.
