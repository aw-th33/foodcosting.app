Brand concept

The visual identity is built on the KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket) / receipt aesthetic — the visual language food business owners encounter hundreds of times a day.

The receipt is the foundation, not a costume.

At the social content level, the receipt metaphor is literal — dashed dividers, monospace numbers, line-item layouts. At the landing page level, it's abstracted into typography rhythm, data presentation, and layout structure. The further from social content, the more refined — but the DNA is always visible.

Rules

  • The f is always lowercase
  • The box always has a visible border (2px at standard size), border-radius 3px
  • The wordmark is always IBM Plex Mono, bold
  • ".app" is always a separate element, smaller, in faded color
  • Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the boxed f mark
  • At very small sizes (16px), use filled variant (white f on ink background)

Absolute rules

Non-negotiable constraints that apply everywhere.

Always

  • Dashed dividers (never solid lines)
  • Monospace for all numbers and data
  • Left-right label:value alignment for data
  • Bold totals at the bottom of data sections
  • Warm paper white (#F8F6F0) as default canvas
  • Signal colors only next to numbers
  • One highlight stamp per slide maximum
  • Sentence case for all copy

Never

  • Gradients, shadows, blur, or glow
  • Colored backgrounds (except dark mode)
  • Serif fonts
  • Decorative elements, illustrations, or emoji
  • Signal colors used decoratively
  • More than two font families
  • Solid divider lines
  • Title Case or ALL CAPS in headlines
