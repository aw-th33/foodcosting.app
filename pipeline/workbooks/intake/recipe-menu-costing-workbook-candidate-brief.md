## Why This Workbook Exists

- Core problem: Small restaurant operators often price dishes using ad hoc spreadsheets that do not properly handle ingredient costs, unit conversion, sub-recipes, and margin targets.
- Why operators care: Pricing errors directly reduce margin, and many teams do not have a clean way to update recipes when ingredient prices change.
- Why now: Competitor research shows strong demand for food cost and recipe costing sheets, but many current options are dated, shallow, or poorly packaged.

## Target User

- Primary user: Owner-operator, kitchen manager, chef, or food business operator
- Business type: Restaurant, cafe, bakery, caterer, food truck
- Skill level: Beginner to intermediate spreadsheet user
- Typical context of use: Menu reviews, recipe costing, price updates, margin checks

## Workbook Job To Be Done

- Main job: Help an operator cost recipes accurately and turn those costs into menu prices using target margin logic.
- Secondary jobs: Standardize ingredient inputs, track sub-recipes, and create a repeatable costing workflow for future dishes.
- What success looks like for the user: They can open the workbook, enter ingredients and recipes, and quickly see per-portion cost, food cost percentage, and suggested menu price.

- Closest competitor examples: Apicbase, RestaurantOwner, 7shifts

- What competitors do poorly: Dated UX, limited onboarding, Excel-first feeling, weak Google Sheets-native polish
- What gap `Foodcosting.app` could own: A modern, approachable, Google Sheets-first workbook that feels useful immediately while still handling real costing complexity
