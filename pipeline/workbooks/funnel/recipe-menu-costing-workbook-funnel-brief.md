# Workbook Funnel Brief

## Workbook Info

- Workbook name: Recipe and Menu Costing Workbook
- Date: 2026-04-25
- Strategist: workbook-funnel-strategist

## Audience

- Primary audience: Owner-operators, chefs, kitchen managers, small food business operators
- Secondary audience: Caterers, bakery owners, food truck operators
- Awareness level: Problem-aware and solution-seeking
- Main pain point: They do not fully trust their current recipe costing and menu pricing process

## Positioning

- Core promise: Know what each dish costs and what to charge without building your own formulas from scratch.
- Supporting promise: Use a practical, Google Sheets-first workbook that helps you price faster and update margins when ingredient costs change.
- Why this is better than a generic template: It is built specifically for recipe costing and menu pricing, not adapted from a generic spreadsheet layout.

## Landing Page Angle

- Primary angle: Free recipe costing workbook for restaurants and food businesses
- Secondary angle: Menu pricing template that helps protect margin
- Suggested headline: Free Recipe Costing and Menu Pricing Workbook for Google Sheets
- Suggested CTA: Open the free workbook

## Delivery Model

- Delivery type: `ungated`
- Reason: This asset is most valuable when users can try it immediately and experience the workbook structure before being asked for anything.
- Recommended format: `Google Sheets copy link | Excel download`

## Free-To-Paid Ladder

1. Free workbook value: Cost recipes, calculate portion cost, and see suggested menu prices
2. Optional next action: Explore related quick sheets like food cost % or yield calculators
3. Free app handoff: Create a free `Foodcosting.app` account to save ingredients and recipes outside the spreadsheet
4. Paid upgrade trigger: Upgrade when recipe count, ingredient changes, locations, or collaboration make spreadsheet maintenance painful

## Upgrade Moments

- Moment 1: Ingredient cost changes affect many recipes at once
- Moment 2: The team needs one shared source of truth instead of duplicate spreadsheets
- Moment 3: The business wants history, consistency, and easier pricing updates

## Related Resource Links

- Related workbook ideas: Inventory and COGS Workbook, Food Cost Percentage Quick Sheet, Yield and Edible Portion Sheet
- Related calculators: food cost %, menu price calculator, yield calculator
- Related blog content: how to calculate food cost, ideal food cost percentage, recipe pricing mistakes

## Risks

- Funnel too weak if: The workbook has no visible next step once users finish their first recipe
- Funnel too aggressive if: The workbook asks for signup before showing value or pushes the app too early
- Messaging risks: Overstating automation would damage trust because the workbook is still manual by nature

## Recommendation

- Recommended packaging approach: Launch as a flagship ungated workbook with a clear landing page, companion quick-sheet links, and light in-workbook upgrade language
- Recommended level of promotion: High
