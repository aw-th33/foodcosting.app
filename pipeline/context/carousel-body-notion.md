## Caption

Your food cost target means nothing if you don't know what it should be.

Ideal food cost is theoretical — exact portions, exact prices, zero waste. A pasta dish at $2.80 cost on a $10 menu price lands at 28%. That's your benchmark. When actual food cost hits 35%, that 7-point gap comes from somewhere: over-portioning, waste, stale recipe prices, or theft. Without the ideal number, you can't tell if the problem is pricing or execution.
